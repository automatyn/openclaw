# LinkedIn post 2026-04-07

Most small businesses trying AI in 2026 are burning money on the wrong part of the setup.

They spend hours picking a model. Comparing pricing. Reading reviews. Then they paste a generic prompt into a chatbot builder and wonder why it sounds like every other bot on the internet.

Here's what actually matters.

An AI agent is not the model. It's the job description you write for it. The difference between a chatbot that feels like a real employee and one that feels like a form is 20 minutes spent writing how it should behave when nobody is watching.

Does it apologize when a customer is rude? Should it.
Does it upsell on every message? It shouldn't.
Does it remember the last conversation with this customer? Only if you told it to save it to disk.

Most setups skip this part entirely. They treat the bot like a search engine. Ask a question, get an answer. Done.

That's why the generic defaults leak through. That's why the bot sounds fake. That's why small businesses try AI once, don't see results, and go back to answering DMs themselves.

The ones who get real results spent the first afternoon writing a personality, a set of rules, and a memory policy. Then they plugged in the model.

The order matters.

What's the first thing you'd teach a new employee if you hired one tomorrow? That's what your bot needs too.
